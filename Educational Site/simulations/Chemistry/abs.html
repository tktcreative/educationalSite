Acids, Bases & pH Scale

Test common household items to determine their pH and see how universal indicator paper changes color.

Understanding pH

The pH scale measures how acidic or basic a substance is. The scale ranges from 0 to 14.

  • A pH of less than 7 indicates an acid.
  • A pH of 7 is neutral.
  • A pH of greater than 7 indicates a base (or alkali).

How to Use:

  • Click on an item from the shelf (e.g., Lemon Juice, Soap).
  • Watch as it's added to the beaker of pure water.
  • Observe the color change of the solution and the pH strip.
  • The pH meter will show you the exact pH value.
  • Click Reset to test another item.
