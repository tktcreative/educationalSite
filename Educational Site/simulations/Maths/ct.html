Circle Theorems

Visualize theorems like "angle at the center is double the angle at the circumference" by dragging points on a circle.

The Theorem

The angle subtended by an arc at the center of a circle is double the angle subtended by it at any point on the remaining part of the circle.

How to Use:

  • The points A and B define an arc on the circle.
  • Click and drag the point P anywhere along the major arc.
  • Observe the relationship between the two angles in the calculations panel.
