What is a Solenoid?

A solenoid is a coil of wire that acts like a magnet when an electric current passes through it. It produces a strong, uniform magnetic field inside the coil.

The strength of the magnetic field (B) is directly proportional to the current (I). The direction of the field can be found using the right-hand grip rule.
